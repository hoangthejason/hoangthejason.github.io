A technical site should make equations readable without turning every page into a PDF surrogate.

## Separating ideas from notation

For binary classification, the decision boundary is often written as

$$
w^T x + b = 0
$$

That notation is compact, but explanation matters more than typography alone.

## A practical rule

State the role of each term in prose before writing the equation. Readers remember semantics better than isolated symbols.

:::tip
Use display math for structure and inline math for local clarification.
:::

## Example

If the sign of $w^T x + b$ determines the predicted class, then the surface defined by

$$
w^T x + b = 0
$$

acts as a separator between the two regions.
